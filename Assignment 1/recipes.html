Yomari Recipe Details

Ingredients Checklists:

    For the Dough:

    • 2 cups of Rice flour
    • 1 tablespoon of ghee
    • Warm water (as needed for soft dough)

    For the Filling:

    • 150 gm of caku (concentrated sugarcane)
    • 3 tablespoons of seasames seeds
    • 2 tablespoons of dry coconut or chopped nuts

Preparation Steps:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the rice flour and ghee. Gradually add warm water while kneading until you get a soft and pliable dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a separate pan, melt the caku over low heat until it becomes a thick syrup. Add the sesame seeds and dry coconut (or chopped nuts) to the syrup and mix well. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. Divide the dough into small equal portions and roll each portion into a ball. Flatten each ball into a small disc using your hands or a rolling pin.
  4. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each disc. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling to form a fish-shaped dumpling, pinching the edges together to seal it.
  5. Arrange the filled yomari in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper or banana leaves to prevent sticking. Steam the yomari for about 15 minutes or until they are cooked through and have a glossy appearance.
  6. Remove the yomari from the steamer and let them cool slightly before serving. Enjoy your delicious homemade yomari!
