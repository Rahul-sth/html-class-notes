Yomari


Yomari is a traditional Newari delicacy from Nepal, often enjoyed during the festival of Yomari Punhi. It is a steamed dumpling made from rice flour dough and filled with a sweet mixture of sesame seeds, molasses, and coconut. The unique shape of Yomari resembles a fish, which symbolizes prosperity and good fortune in Newar culture. This treat is not only delicious but also holds cultural significance, making it a cherished part of Nepalese culinary heritage.
